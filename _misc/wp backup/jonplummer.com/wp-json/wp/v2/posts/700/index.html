We should<\/em> provide alternate content for anything that is not text or cannot be read by a screen reader and that is important to the meaning of the page \u2013 for example, if the image were missing or broken the page\u2019s meaning or intelligibility would be diminished \u2013 and should not<\/em> provide alternate content for anything that can be safely left out without diminishing that meaning.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

There are a few ways to provide alternate content; the\u00a0alt<\/code>\u00a0attribute is one of them. It appears that\u00a0gov.uk<\/a>\u00a0is expressing in part that the\u00a0alt<\/code>\u00a0attribute is a less accessible way of presenting that alternate content than simply putting that alternate content right into the page, and that\u2019s true: a person who needs the content in the\u00a0alt<\/code>\u00a0attribute but doesn’t use a screen reader (such as a person using a magnifier) will have to do some extra work to dig that content out of the\u00a0alt<\/code>\u00a0attribute that a screen reader user would not need to do.
