Expectations of myself<\/h2>\n\n\n\n
  • Foster an ethos of continuous improvement<\/li>
  • Repeatedly return to user needs and goals to create alignment<\/li>
  • Frame design goals as the marriage of user goals and business wishes<\/li>
  • Exhibit genuine care for each person I support<\/li>
  • Use wisdom and humor when shaping behavior<\/li>
  • Model desired behavior and attitudes for others<\/li>
  • Address what’s needed wherever it appears<\/li><\/ul>\n\n\n\n

    Expectations of designers<\/h2>\n\n\n\n
    • Scenario-based, goal-oriented<\/li>
    • Use storyboards to start understanding a scenario<\/li>
    • Freely move between storyboard, workflow, wires, prototype as needed to tackle problems<\/li>
    • Make small prototypes to test and learn<\/li>
    • The prioritization and arrangement of data directs user attention and action<\/li>
    • Organize “done” work into bite-size chunks, set apart from exploratory work<\/li><\/ul>\n\n\n\n

      Expectations of all workers<\/h2>\n\n\n\n
      • Work to make the job of the next person easier<\/li>
      • Work in public, out in the open<\/li>
      • Go and see<\/li>
      • Be aware of and have your work informed by user goals and business needs together<\/li>
      • Create possibilities cheaply, winnow according to goals, improve the best ones<\/li>
      • Involve others in solving problems, chewing on possibilities<\/li>
      • When presenting possibilities, arrive with your own evaluation of each one \u2013 pros and cons, fit to purpose, what you like and don\u2019t like \u2013 and the sort of feedback you need<\/li>
      • Scope is negotiated \u2013 watch for improvements AND for lighter interventions<\/li>
      • Experiment with possible improvements to your own and collective work<\/li><\/ul>\n\n\n\n
