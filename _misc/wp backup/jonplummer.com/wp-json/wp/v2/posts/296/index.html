If you search the web for “double diamond<\/a>” you’ll find a great many articles describing a design or product management or product development process in which you<\/p>\n\n\n\n

    \n
  • choose what to build\n
      \n
    • discover \u2013 gather insight into the problem area(s)<\/li>\n\n\n\n
    • define \u2013 express the area to focus on as a distinct selected problem<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n<\/li>\n\n\n\n
    • then build it well\n
        \n
      • develop \u2013 generate potential solutions<\/li>\n\n\n\n
      • deliver \u2013 make and ship a potential solution<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n\n\n\n

        Some people call this “build the right thing” and “build the thing right.” “Discover” and “develop” are meant to be generative, expansive processes, and “define” and “deliver” are meant to be specifying\/selecting\/contracting processes. This puts an expansion and a contraction in each of two phases, hence the diamond shapes. There are two phases, hence “double diamond.”<\/p>\n\n\n\n
