I was fielding complaints about slowness in the UX team’s contribution to projects until I started talking to the people I support about this concept. Turns out it is good for anyone who works on a team, anyone who works with others.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

When starting on or working on a project, I expect everyone to:<\/p>\n\n\n\n

    \n
  • come to a project with a plan, <\/li>\n\n\n\n
  • share and negotiate that plan, and <\/li>\n\n\n\n
  • be conspicuous in fulfilling that plan, by\n
      \n
    • regularly offering work-in-progress for discussion (there’s no such thing as “not ready to look at yet”),<\/li>\n\n\n\n
    • regularly offering the status of current work,<\/li>\n\n\n\n
    • regularly predicting when the current activity will be done,<\/li>\n\n\n\n
    • allowing for the renegotiation of the plan, and <\/li>\n\n\n\n
    • offering modifications to the plan as project or business conditions change (active renegotiation).<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n\n\n\n

      You’ll note that the default mode here is active \u2013 actively planning, actively sharing, actively predicting, actively responding to change.<\/p>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
