A recent correspondent asked how to move his team from “thoughts and fears about plans” to action, and I suggestion making their next intervention a pilot<\/em>. That way an intervention is an experiment<\/em>, and can be evaluated, then continued, tweaked, or ended.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

I counseled him to consider coaching folks to articulate<\/p>\n\n\n\n

  • the problem we see<\/li>
  • the result we want<\/li>
  • what we’ll try, for how long<\/li>
  • how we will know if it is working or not<\/li>
  • how we will know if we should stop the pilot early<\/li><\/ul>\n\n\n\n

    At the end of the pilot you can evaluate the results and decide to<\/p>\n\n\n\n

    • continue as-is<\/li>
    • continue with tweaks<\/li>
    • try something else<\/li>
    • stop altogether<\/li><\/ul>\n\n\n\n

      with the same five points above for any change or new intervention.<\/p>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
